5 QUESTIONS THAT YOU SHOULD BE ASKING YOUR REALTOR WHEN SEARCHING FOR YOUR RENTAL PROPERTY?

Date by Ryan Gravel
  • Category

Having been in the real estate industry for 15+ years now I have had my fair share of questions asked by clients. Some extremely intelligent, others seemingly witty and some oddly creepy ones as well … So what are the right questions to ask your agent and why should you be asking them?

1) Are you a licensed real estate agent?

I cannot stress it enough how important it is that you are working with a licensed agent. Although the times are changing here in Mexico there are still many unlicensed agents practicing real estate here in Mexico. Ideally you want to look for an agent that has a license in both the country they are from ex) USA (NAR) and Canada (CREA) along with a license from a Mexican association (CEDUVI) (AMPI). This insures you are being represented by a professional that has had the proper training and hold _____ knowledge of the buying process.
The laws and regulations specific to the Riviera Maya differ from many other parts of Mexico. This will insure that the necessary steps are taken during the pre and post purchasing process

“If I had an hour to solve a problem and my life depended on it I would use the first 55 minutes determining the right questions to ask”

Albert Einstein

2) What is your real estate experience?

Even if your realtor is licensed they may still be lacking in experience. One may think an agent's experience is just how many years he has been in the business, however it is much more, especially here in the Riviera Maya.
You will want to choose a realtor who is a seasoned veteran in this specific market. They will know the lay of the land, the hot spots, new and upcoming developments and have an established history and track record of sales. They will be able to steer you away from problem areas, properties, and developers.
They have been around long enough to see who is delivering their promises and who is over selling. Finally, the well established vets in the local market have established relationships with the developers go a long way.
I know for myself, I am offered special deals for my clients that are not offered to all the other agents in the area. This can save you x amount of dollars or allow you to get in before on friends and family deals before they go public.
Remember you when you are working with a professional that has dedicated the last 10 years of their life to their real estate career you know they will always be around hence available to you. This is a very transient market, you do not want to be left hanging by a rookie realtor that needed to make a couple of bucks to go traveling.


3) How will you keep me informed and how often?

Purchasing real estate abroad or even nationally but in a different state you will want to stay informed throughout the process. When you are actively searching for real estate either abroad you need someone with boots on the ground who is going to be in constant contact. They need to have your best interests in mind all the while keeping you informed. It is important your agent fully understands your goals from day one, the more clear you are with your criteria, wants, and needs the better for all parties involved. Communication is the #1 factor in keeping you at ease throughout the buying process. When purchasing property in Mexico there are formalities you may not be accustomed to and time tables that may either be faster or slower than you are used to. The communication between you and your agent will be the main factor in a transparent and smooth experience throughout the buying process. The transparency should start from day one of your search and continue to post follow up. You will notice very quickly from the beginning of your search if your agents attentiveness, follow up and attention to detail meets your standards.


4) Can you send me a referrals of your previous clients?

After you have asked the above questions and feel comfortable moving forward your next question is to ask your agent for a couple of references.
They should have no problem handing over a couple of names and numbers to you. Don't be concerned about contacting these referrals, I have yet to have one client . Your agent of course should first ask permission from their clients before contacting you.
No one is going to turn you down if you call them and ask how the service was from their realtor.
The referrals will be able to tell you how the agent performed through the sales process pre and post sale follow up and what their experience was like with them. They will be able to answer questions like Were they accessible? On time? Reliable? Did they keep you up to date periodically? Would they recommend them to others?

5) What returns are your clients currently seeing?

Now that you have asked the right questions to make sure your realtor is experienced, you have spoken to their previous clients and they have given a positive review the next question you must ask your agent is what returns their clients are currently seeing. This will give you a good idea of realistic returns, NOT projected returns. It solidifies your investment decision with legitimacy in rental history, occupancy rates and NET returns on investment. You will want to ask your agent to put you in touch with their clients who have a similar investment strategy as you do by following this criteria:

  • · Location
  • · Purchase Price
  • · Number of days used by Owner
  • · Time of the year used by Owner
  • · Long term or Vacation Rentals

From my experience when you speak to a referral they open up like a book however you do not want to be too intrusive. I would stick to asking questions that will give you round figures not exact, when it comes to people's personal finances they are typically a bit reserved. Try going with these questions:

  • · What is your average yearly occupancy?
  • · What ADR (Average Daily Rate) is your property showing in peak season?
  • · What ADR is your property showing in low season?
  • · Are you receiving the projected returns on the project? More? Less?

So now the ball is in your court go out there and find yourself an awesome realtor and that great rental property.
