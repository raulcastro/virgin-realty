Tulum Ruins, Riviera Maya

Part of what makes the Riviera Maya such a great destination is the abundance of cultural activities available. From cooking classes to historical sites, culture vultures have their appetites well and truly satisfied.The Mayan ruins are synonymous with the Riviera Maya and its rich culture.
Attracting millions of visitors a year, the Tulum ruins are a true gem of the Riviera Maya and they are finally being recognised as such.
Generally, Chichen Itza is considered to be the most famous historical site on the Yucatan Peninsula, and one of the most famous in Mexico. So it is this Mayan site that is usually chosen to feature in Tripadvisor’s top lists. However, for the first time this year, the Tulum ruins have been chosen instead.
What attracts many visitors to the Tulum ruins above others is their uniqueness. First of all, they are thought to be one of the only well-preserved mesoamerican ruins located on a beach front. The Tulum ruins are perched on a clifftop overlooking the Caribbean sea which is 12 meters below. This makes for some stunning photo opportunities with rocky outcrops, sugary sand, azul sea and majestic ruins all in one! Moreover, before it’s current name, the city was called Zama or City of Dawn as this is the first place that the sunrise would reach in the Mayan world.
Eventually, the city was named Tulum to mean ‘wall’ as the Mayan site was an important port but also a strong fort in it’s lifetime during the years 1200 to 1450.
Nowadays, the Tulum ruins are the perfect day tour in the Riviera Maya. Daily tours start from sunrise, when all is quiet and you can really appreciate where the name Zama came from. Then the crowds descend and the ruins are momentarily repopulated until sunset.
