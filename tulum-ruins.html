Tulum Ruins

Are One of Top 25 Landmarks in the World

Part of what makes the Riviera Maya such a great destination is the abundance of cultural activities available. From cooking classes to historical sites, culture vultures have their appetites well and truly satisfied.The Mayan ruins are synonymous with the Riviera Maya and its rich culture. Attracting millions of visitors a year, the Tulum ruins are a true gem of the Riviera Maya and they are finally being recognised as such.

TripAdvisor’s Top 25 Landmarks in the World 2017

Tripadvisor has recently released their list of top 25 landmarks in the entire world. And for the first time, the Tulum ruins are being featured!
The top spot was taken by Angkor Wat in Siem Reap, Cambodia. This Indianised temple complex is the largest religious monument in the world. Other notable landmarks included Saint Peter’s Basilica in Vatican City, Italy as well as Machu Picchu in Peru and Golden Gate Bridge in San Francisco, USA. But for the first time, the Tulum ruins have crept into the 24th spot.

Tulum Ruins, Riviera Maya

Generally, Chichen Itza is considered to be the most famous historical site on the Yucatan Peninsula, and one of the most famous in Mexico. So it is this Mayan site that is usually chosen to feature in Tripadvisor’s top lists. However, for the first time this year, the Tulum ruins have been chosen instead.
What attracts many visitors to the Tulum ruins above others is their uniqueness. First of all, they are thought to be one of the only well-preserved mesoamerican ruins located on a beach front. The Tulum ruins are perched on a clifftop overlooking the Caribbean sea which is 12 meters below. This makes for some stunning photo opportunities with rocky outcrops, sugary sand, azul sea and majestic ruins all in one! Moreover, before it’s current name, the city was called Zama or City of Dawn as this is the first place that the sunrise would reach in the Mayan world. Eventually, the city was named Tulum to mean ‘wall’ as the Mayan site was an important port but also a strong fort in it’s lifetime during the years 1200 to 1450.

Tulum Ruins

Nowadays, the Tulum ruins are the perfect day tour in the Riviera Maya. Daily tours start from sunrise, when all is quiet and you can really appreciate where the name Zama came from. Then the crowds descend and the ruins are momentarily repopulated until sunset.

Recognition for Riviera Maya

It’s widely known that the Riviera Maya is an established yet growing tourism hotspot. However recognition such as the Tulum ruins being included in the top 25 landmarks worldwide help to continuously develop the Riviera Maya’s reputation as a world-class travel destination.

Tulum Investment Opportunities

This recognition only helps to increase the opportunities for investment within the area. Due to it’s unique character, exceptionally preserved nature and world-renowned attractions, Tulum is on an upwards trajectory that seems to know no bounds. Though this little fishing village has already grown into the ‘next big thing’ in travel, Tulum is nowhere near reaching its peak. Average annual appreciation in Tulum between the years 2013 and 2017 was 11.98%. As a result, it’s currently one of the most successful investment areas in Mexico.
